Finding the beginner's mind

Imposter Syndrome. Yeah, that thing. I know it too well…

I am tenacious, and because of that I have failed over and over and over again in my life. But my biggest challenge isn’t finding success, it is letting go as an opportunity for true awakening.

Pema Chodron, a Buddhist teacher, has a famous saying “only to the extent that we expose ourselves over and over to annihilation can that which is indestructible be found in us.”

Society unfortunately instills the purpose is to pass the test or to overcome the problem. Truth is, things don’t really get solved. They come together again and fall apart again.

The healing comes from letting there be room for things to exist.

Just be here and acknowledge that thing.

Shunryū Suzuki writes, “…you should not be bothered by the various images you find in your mind. Let them come and let them go. Then they will be under control.” Just like passing clouds or a log flowing down a river, the dominating power in consciousness will diminish.

My daily reminder: Start my day where I am and to listen with a receptive heart. It is there I find myself, not the imposter.
